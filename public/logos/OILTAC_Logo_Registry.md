# OILTAC Logo Registry

A registry of every independent testing institution, laboratory, government body, or organization that conducted or commissioned an OILTAC (or predecessor-named "Super Jox" / "PEAM" / "TAC") fuel additive test, as documented in `/OILTAC-CLAUDE/References/OILTAC_Test_Registry.md` and `/OILTAC-CLAUDE/References/OILTAC_RAG_Reference.md`. OILTAC itself, its manufacturer (Fukasawa Chemical Laboratory Inc.), and commercial distributors (Hino Shoji Ltd., Ocean Chemicals Inc.) are excluded.

Logos were retrieved from the institution's own website where possible, otherwise from Wikimedia Commons (which attaches its own licensing). Where an institution no longer exists under its original name, the successor organization's current logo was retrieved and that fact is noted under "Notes." Where no clear official logo could be located, the entry is marked "not found" and no substitute was provided.

Registry compiled: 2026-04-24.

### 2. Engineering Institute of Transportation Ministry, Japan

- **Country:** Japan
- **Type:** Government research institution
- **Logo filename:** `ship-research-institute-nmri.svg`
- **Source URL:** https://www.nmri.go.jp/en/images/logo_e.svg (linked from https://www.nmri.go.jp/en/)
- **Notes:** This 1983 institution name is a translation variant of the same Ministry of Transport ship-engineering research body that later appears as "Marine Technical Institute / Ship Research Institute" in the 1985–86 record (entry 9 below). The institute is now known as the **National Maritime Research Institute (NMRI)** under the National Institute of Maritime, Port and Aviation Technology (MPAT, formed 2016). The NMRI English-site logo is used here as the successor organization's official logo. Official successor source.
- **Tests:** Test 2 (AVL 502 Bunker C bench test, 1983)

### 4. Fuel Experimental Station, San Donato Milanese

- **Country:** Italy
- **Type:** Research institution / experimental station (now part of a Chamber-of-Commerce-owned innovation centre)
- **Logo filename:** `innovhub-stazione-sperimentale-combustibili.jpg`
- **Source URL:** https://www.innovhub-ssi.it/imgpub/1969343/0/0/logo-innovhub.jpg (linked from https://www.innovhub-ssi.it/)
- **Notes:** The "Stazione Sperimentale per i Combustibili" (SSC), founded 1940 in San Donato Milanese, is now the Combustibili Division of **Innovhub – Stazioni Sperimentali per l'Industria Srl**, owned by the Chamber of Commerce of Milan Monza Brianza Lodi. The logo retrieved is Innovhub's current corporate logo (500 × 173 px JPEG). Format note: a vector version was not exposed on the public site. Official successor source.
- **Tests:** Test 4 (Alfetta 1600 / Fiat 850 S gasoline efficacy, 1982); Test 5 (Diesel efficacy, 1982)

### 9. Marine Technical Institute / Ship Research Institute, Transportation Ministry of Japan

- **Country:** Japan
- **Type:** Government research institution (under the Ministry of Transport)
- **Logo filename:** `ship-research-institute-nmri.svg`
- **Source URL:** https://www.nmri.go.jp/en/images/logo_e.svg (linked from https://www.nmri.go.jp/en/)
- **Notes:** Same institution as entry 2 above (recorded under different English translations in the source documents). The Ship Research Institute (船舶技術研究所), located at 6-38-1 Shinkawa, Mitaka City, Tokyo, became the **National Maritime Research Institute (NMRI)** in 2001 and is now part of MPAT. The NMRI English-site logo is reused here. Official successor source. The same logo file is referenced from both entries.
- **Tests:** Test 10 (200-hour AVL-502 long-term test on A/C blend fuel, 1985–1986)

### 12. JR (Japan Railways) — JR Group

- **Country:** Japan
- **Type:** National operator (group of regional successor railway companies to Japanese National Railways, post-1987 privatization)
- **Logo filename:** `japan-railways-group.svg`
- **Source URL:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:JR_logo_JRgroup.svg
- **Notes:** Generic JR Group logomark (designed by Yoji Yamamoto, Yusuke Kaiji and Kazumasa Nagai of Nippon Design Center). The OILTAC product brochure refers to a multi-year diesel rail-car field trial conducted by "JR" without specifying a single regional company; the JR Group mark is therefore the closest correct match. 300 × 162.6 viewBox SVG. Source: Wikimedia Commons (not a JR-company website).
- **Tests:** JR diesel rail car multi-year field trial referenced in oiltac brochure ("several percent saving of fuel was confirmed").
